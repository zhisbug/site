Biography

I am currently a graduate student in the Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University. My advisor is Prof. Eric Xing.
I received the M.S. degree from Computer Science and Engineer Department in Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2014, under the supervision of Prof. Liqing Zhang. Before that, I completed my Bachelor's degree from 2008 to 2011 at South China University of Technology, majoring in Computer Science.

Research Interest

My research interest is in the intersection area of machine learning, distributed system and computer vision.

Software

  • GeePS: a GPU-specialized Parameter Server.
    GeePS provides a GPU-specialized parameter server for scalable deep learning on distributed GPUs.
  • Poseidon: Distributed Deep Learning on CPU/GPU Clusters.
    A scalable open-source framework for large-scale distributed deep learning on CPU/GPU clusters.
  • Petuum: Distributed Machine Learning Framework.
    Petuum provides a generic algorithmic and systems interface to large scale machine learning.
  • HD-CNN: Hierarchical Deep Convolutional Neural Network for Large Scale Visual Recognition.
    HD-CNN is a generic and principled hierarchical architecture that enables you to incorporate category heirarchies into your CNN training.
  • Automatic Content-aware Photo Enhancer.
    A context-aware photo enhancer based on deep neural networks.
